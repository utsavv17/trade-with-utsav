My Strategies

Strangle

A strangle strategy is similar to a straddle as both rely on the volatility of the market. It is also made with the purchase of a call and put option. However, a strangle cost less than a straddle. This is because the put option has a lower strike price and the call option has a higher strike price.

Straddle

Investors use the straddle strategy when they believe the price of the underlying asset will be volatile. This is made with a call and put option with the same strike price. If premiums are not considered, then investors will receive a profit if the spot price is either bigger or smaller than the strike price..

Buy Today Sell Tomorrow (BTST)

BTST (Buy Today Sell Tomorrow) trading is Short-term trading where traders take advantage of short-term fluctuations in share prices. Unlike intraday trade, where shares are bought and sold the same day, shares in BTST are sold either- in cash or futures and options category and are sold the next day.

Calendar spreads

A calendar spread is an options or futures strategy established by simultaneously entering a long and short position on the same underlying asset but with different delivery dates. In a typical calendar spread, one would buy a longer-term contract and go short a nearer-term option with the same strike price.
